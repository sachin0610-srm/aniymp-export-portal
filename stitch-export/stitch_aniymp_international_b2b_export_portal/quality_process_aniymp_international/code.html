Export Preparation Built Around Traceability

ANIYMP INTERNATIONAL follows a practical workflow for spice sourcing, receiving, inspection, cleaning, sorting, drying, grading, packing, storage, dispatch, and traceability. The page avoids unverified technical claims and presents process controls in buyer-friendly language.

Buyer assurance

Documentation, supplier records, batch details, and relevant product reports can be shared with qualified buyers on request.

14-Step Export Process

A clear operational sequence from supplier sourcing to final traceability.

Request Process Details
01

Supplier sourcing

Identify suitable suppliers and procurement channels for required spice products.

02

Raw material receiving

Receive raw materials with basic documentation and lot information.

03

Incoming inspection

Review appearance, condition, supplier details, and buyer-specific requirements.

04

Cleaning

Remove unwanted foreign matter through suitable cleaning practices.

05

Sorting

Separate materials by visual condition, size, form, and product suitability.

06

Drying / moisture control

Support stable handling and storage through controlled drying practices where relevant.

07

Grading / Lab Analysis

Classify product lots and support buyer-required lab analysis where applicable.

08

Processing / Fumigation

Prepare product form and coordinate fumigation requirements according to shipment needs.

09

Packing

Pack materials according to agreed packaging preference and destination needs.

10

Batch identification

Maintain lot references to support traceability and buyer documentation.

11

Storage

Store packed product in suitable dry, clean, and organized conditions.

12

Final inspection

Review packing, labels, documents, and buyer instructions before dispatch.

13

Dispatch

Coordinate shipment preparation and handover according to the agreed route.

14

Traceability

Retain records to support product history, buyer review, and non-conformity handling.
